Guidelines for the Writing Task:
  • Part 1 (Present Continuous Tense Focus):
    Write about the current atmosphere of the event. Include detailed descriptions of ongoing activities, live interviews, the responses of key figures, and the reactions of the crowd. Ensure you use the present continuous tense consistently to describe all ongoing actions.
    Example: The speakers are addressing the audience, and the participants are actively engaging in discussions.
  • Part 2 (Past Continuous Tense Focus):
    Reflect on the events leading up to the present. Describe how the situation has evolved and what people were doing at critical points before the event began. Use the past continuous tense to emphasise ongoing past actions.
    Example: Last night, the organisers were making final preparations, while the media was setting up the equipment.

Key Requirements:
  • At least 250 words for each part (present and past).
  • Ensure smooth transitions between past and present events.
